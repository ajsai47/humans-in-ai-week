# Track 2: AI and the Future of Work

## The question

What happens to jobs, careers, and economic opportunity when AI can do most knowledge work?

## Opening framing (2 minutes — read or adapt)

> Every major technology shift has reshaped work. The printing press, the steam engine, the internet — each one destroyed jobs and created new ones. But AI is different in one critical way: it's not replacing physical labor. It's replacing cognitive labor. The thing we told everyone to go to college for.
>
> In the next 5 years, AI will change how we write, code, analyze, design, sell, diagnose, and manage. Some jobs will disappear. Many more will transform. New ones will emerge that don't exist yet.
>
> Tonight we're not here to panic or to celebrate. We're here to get honest about what's coming and how we prepare.

## Discussion questions

### 1. The personal question
"How has AI already changed your work? Not hypothetically — specifically. What do you do differently today than you did a year ago?"

**Moderator tip**: Start here. Ground the conversation in lived experience, not headlines.

### 2. The displacement question
"Which jobs or industries in [your city] are most vulnerable to AI automation in the next 3–5 years? Which ones are most protected?"

**Moderator tip**: Make this local. A logistics hub will answer differently than a university town.

### 3. The skills question
"If you were entering the workforce today at 22, what would you learn? What advice would you ignore?"

**Moderator tip**: Great for mixed-age audiences. Let people disagree — the tension is the point.

### 4. The inequality question
"AI tools cost money and require skills to use well. Is AI going to widen the gap between people who can adapt and people who can't? What do we do about it?"

**Moderator tip**: This is where the conversation gets real. Don't let it stay abstract — push for specific proposals.

### 5. The meaning question
"If AI handles most of the productive work, what do humans do? Is work the center of our identity, or is that changing?"

**Moderator tip**: Philosophical closer. Some people will say UBI, others will say new kinds of work. Both are valid.

## Localization prompt

> "In [city], the dominant industries are [X, Y, Z]. AI is already [specific impact — e.g., 'automating supply chain logistics' / 'changing how startups build products' / 'raising questions about what our university should be teaching']. So when we talk about the future of work, we're talking about the future of *this city*."

## Audience participation ideas

- **Live poll**: "On a scale of 1–5, how worried are you about AI affecting your job?" (show of hands or app-based)
- **Breakout pairs**: After question 1, pair people from different industries for a 3-minute exchange
- **Commitment close**: "Name one thing you're going to do differently at work next week because of this conversation"
