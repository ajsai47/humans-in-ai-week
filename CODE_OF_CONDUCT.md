# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Humans in AI Week is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion, or technology choices.

## Our standards

**Expected behavior:**
- Be respectful and considerate in speech and actions
- Listen actively and make space for diverse perspectives
- Welcome newcomers and people from non-technical backgrounds
- Provide constructive feedback when discussing AI — avoid dismissiveness
- Respect the Chatham House Rule when applied: share what was said, not who said it

**Unacceptable behavior:**
- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Hate speech, slurs, or derogatory comments
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Deliberate disruption of events or discussions
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Commercial pitching or recruiting during discussion sessions (save it for networking time)

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all HAIW events — in-person and virtual — across all 200+ participating cities.

## Enforcement

Chapter leads are responsible for enforcing this code of conduct at their local events. If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **At an event:** Speak to the chapter lead or event organizer immediately
2. **After an event:** Email catherine@aicollective.com

Reports will be reviewed and addressed promptly. Anonymity of reporters will be protected.
