# Production Checklist

Everything needed to produce a flagship HAIW conference. Work backwards from event day.

---

## 10 weeks out

### Venue
- [ ] Identify 3–5 venue options (capacity 1,500–2,000, full-day availability)
- [ ] Site visits — check: main stage, breakout rooms (3), exhibition hall, registration area, green room, catering space
- [ ] Confirm A/V capabilities (built-in vs. bring your own)
- [ ] Negotiate contract — key terms: full-day access (7 AM load-in to 8 PM teardown), catering exclusivity?, WiFi capacity, parking
- [ ] Sign venue contract

### Team
- [ ] Assign city lead (overall owner)
- [ ] Assign production manager (day-of logistics)
- [ ] Assign speaker liaison
- [ ] Assign sponsor liaison
- [ ] Assign volunteer coordinator

---

## 8 weeks out

### Speakers
- [ ] Headliner invites sent (see [Speaker Strategy](./speaker-strategy.md))
- [ ] Main stage speaker invites sent
- [ ] Moderators identified and confirmed for each panel

### Sponsorship
- [ ] Title sponsor confirmed
- [ ] Presenting sponsor outreach complete
- [ ] Supporting / exhibitor outreach underway

### Ticketing
- [ ] Luma event page live with all ticket tiers
- [ ] Super early bird tickets on sale
- [ ] Comp tickets allocated (speakers, press, partners)

---

## 6 weeks out

### Content
- [ ] All main stage panels finalized (topic, speakers, moderator)
- [ ] Breakout session formats confirmed
- [ ] Lightning talk speakers selected
- [ ] Demo showcase companies selected (6–8)
- [ ] Agenda published on event page

### Marketing
- [ ] First announcement post (social + newsletter)
- [ ] Speaker announcement graphics created
- [ ] Press list built (local + national tech media)
- [ ] Media pitch emails sent

### Production
- [ ] A/V vendor contracted (sound, lighting, screens, livestream)
- [ ] Signage vendor identified (banners, stage backdrop, directional signs)
- [ ] Catering menu confirmed (breakfast, lunch, snacks, reception)
- [ ] Photography + videography booked

---

## 4 weeks out

### Speakers
- [ ] All speakers confirmed (no open slots)
- [ ] Headshots + bios collected from every speaker
- [ ] Speakers page updated
- [ ] Panel questions drafted and shared with moderators

### Sponsorship
- [ ] All sponsor logos collected (vector format)
- [ ] Sponsor booth layout finalized
- [ ] Sponsor deliverables confirmed (what they're bringing, shipping logistics)

### Marketing
- [ ] Second wave social push — speaker spotlights
- [ ] Newsletter countdown segment
- [ ] Press follow-ups — confirm media attendees
- [ ] Ticket sales check — if below target, increase promotion

### Logistics
- [ ] Volunteer team recruited (target: 15–25 volunteers)
- [ ] Volunteer roles assigned: registration, speaker liaison, sponsor support, room monitors, A/V, wayfinding
- [ ] Badge design finalized (attendee name, company, ticket tier)
- [ ] Badge printing ordered

---

## 2 weeks out

### Speakers
- [ ] Panel questions sent to all speakers
- [ ] Co-panelist intros made (email introduction between panelists)
- [ ] Travel/hotel confirmed for out-of-town speakers
- [ ] Run of show shared with all speakers (their session time, duration, stage location)

### Production
- [ ] Final venue walkthrough — confirm room setup, A/V positions, sponsor booths, signage placement
- [ ] WiFi stress-tested (can it handle 1,500 devices?)
- [ ] Livestream test — end-to-end (camera → encoder → platform → viewer)
- [ ] Signage files sent to printer
- [ ] Afterparty venue confirmed and logistics finalized

### Marketing
- [ ] "2 weeks out" social push
- [ ] Final PR pitch — "200 cities, same week — [City] hosts 1,500-person flagship"
- [ ] Attendee email: what to expect, venue address, parking, schedule

---

## 1 week out

- [ ] Final ticket count reviewed — waitlist if near capacity
- [ ] All signage printed and ready
- [ ] Badges printed
- [ ] Sponsor materials received and sorted
- [ ] Catering final count submitted to venue
- [ ] Volunteer briefing scheduled (day before event)
- [ ] Speaker reminder email sent (date, time, arrival time, green room location)
- [ ] Day-of run sheet finalized (minute by minute, including transitions)
- [ ] Emergency plan reviewed (medical, weather, fire exit routes)

---

## Day before

- [ ] Load-in: signage, banners, sponsor booths, registration setup
- [ ] A/V setup and sound check
- [ ] Livestream dry run
- [ ] Registration test (badge scanning, check-in flow)
- [ ] Green room setup (water, coffee, phone chargers, mirror, schedule on wall)
- [ ] Volunteer briefing (roles, schedule, escalation contacts)
- [ ] Walk the full attendee journey: entrance → registration → main stage → breakouts → lunch → exhibition → reception

---

## Day of

### Before doors open (7:00 – 8:00 AM)
- [ ] Team arrives at 7:00 AM
- [ ] Final A/V check on all stages
- [ ] Sponsor booths confirmed ready
- [ ] Registration stations powered on and tested
- [ ] Signage checked (nothing fallen, nothing missing)
- [ ] Green room stocked and labeled
- [ ] Photographer arrives and is briefed
- [ ] Livestream go/no-go at 8:00 AM

### During event (8:00 AM – 7:00 PM)
- [ ] Registration team in position by 8:00 AM
- [ ] Stage manager cues speakers 10 minutes before each session
- [ ] Moderator receives 5-minute and 2-minute time warnings
- [ ] Photographer captures: crowd, speakers, sponsors, candids, venue
- [ ] Social media team posts in real-time (key quotes, photos, behind-the-scenes)
- [ ] Monitor attendee feedback — address issues in real-time
- [ ] Lunch: check catering timing, signage to dining area, dietary options visible

### After event (7:00 PM onward)
- [ ] Thank all speakers personally
- [ ] Teardown: signage, sponsor materials, A/V strike
- [ ] Venue walk-through with venue manager (any damage? all clear?)
- [ ] Secure all equipment and materials

---

## Post-event (within 1 week)

- [ ] Send thank-you emails: speakers, sponsors, volunteers, venue
- [ ] Share photos with speakers and sponsors (within 48 hours)
- [ ] Post event recap on social with top photos and quotes
- [ ] Send attendee follow-up email (recap, photos, community links)
- [ ] Send sponsor recap reports
- [ ] Submit post-event data to AIC global team
- [ ] Debrief with city lead team: what worked, what didn't, what to change
- [ ] Financial reconciliation: revenue vs. expenses
