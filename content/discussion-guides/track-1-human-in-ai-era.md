# Track 1: What It Means to Be Human in the AI Era

## The question

What makes us human when machines can think, create, and communicate?

## Opening framing (2 minutes — read or adapt)

> For the first time in history, we have machines that can write poetry, compose music, diagnose disease, and hold conversations that feel real. This isn't science fiction — it's Tuesday.
>
> But here's the thing: the more capable AI becomes, the more important the question becomes — what is uniquely, irreducibly human? What do we want to protect? What do we want to let go of? And what does it mean to live a meaningful life alongside intelligence that isn't alive?
>
> Tonight we're not here to predict the future. We're here to ask what we want to *keep*.

## Discussion questions

Use 3–5 of these. Let the conversation breathe — don't rush through all of them.

### 1. The mirror question
"AI can now do many things we thought required human intelligence — writing, art, analysis, even empathy. Has this changed how you think about what makes you *you*?"

**Moderator tip**: This is a warm-up. Let people share personal reflections. Don't debate — collect perspectives.

### 2. The creativity question
"When an AI can generate a painting, a poem, or a song — is that creativity? Does it matter if it is?"

**Moderator tip**: This one gets heated. Anchor it in *what creativity means to the speaker*, not abstract philosophy.

### 3. The connection question
"We now have AI companions, AI therapists, AI tutors. If an AI interaction *feels* meaningful, is it? What's lost or gained when we form relationships with machines?"

**Moderator tip**: No judgment on people who use AI for companionship. The question is about what we're choosing and why.

### 4. The agency question
"AI increasingly makes recommendations about what we read, watch, buy, and believe. How do we stay in the driver's seat of our own lives?"

**Moderator tip**: Good for audience participation — most people have a personal story about algorithmic influence.

### 5. The next generation question
"For children growing up right now, AI will be as natural as electricity. What do we need to teach them that we never had to learn?"

**Moderator tip**: Strong closer. Parents in the room will light up. Let it be hopeful.

## Localization prompt

Adapt this opener for your city:

> "In [city], we [specific local context — e.g., 'are a city built by artists and makers' / 'have one of the highest AI adoption rates in the country' / 'are watching factories automate faster than anywhere in the region']. So when we ask what it means to be human in the AI era, we're not asking in the abstract. We're asking because it's already happening here."

## Audience participation ideas

- **Show of hands**: "How many of you have used AI in the last 24 hours?" (opens with shared experience)
- **Turn and talk**: After question 3, have people discuss with the person next to them for 2 minutes before opening to the room
- **One-word close**: At the end, go around and ask each person for one word that captures how they feel leaving tonight
