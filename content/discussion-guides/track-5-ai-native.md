# Track 5: How to Be AI Native

## The question

How do you integrate AI into your daily life and work — not as a novelty, but as a core capability?

## Opening framing (2 minutes — read or adapt)

> There's a growing divide between people who use AI every day and people who've tried it once and shrugged. It's not about intelligence. It's about fluency.
>
> Being "AI native" doesn't mean you're a programmer. It means you know how to think with AI — when to use it, when not to, how to prompt it, how to evaluate its output, and how to make yourself more capable because of it.
>
> Tonight is practical. We're going to share what's actually working in our daily lives and help each other level up.

## Discussion questions

### 1. The daily use question
"Walk us through your last 24 hours. Where did AI show up? Where did you choose not to use it?"

**Moderator tip**: This normalizes AI use. You'll hear everything from "I used it to draft 10 emails" to "I asked it to explain my kid's homework." All valid.

### 2. The learning curve question
"What's the biggest misconception you had about AI before you started using it regularly? What changed?"

**Moderator tip**: Great for mixed-experience audiences. Beginners learn from power users' early mistakes.

### 3. The workflow question
"What's one AI workflow that saves you serious time or makes you significantly better at your job? Be specific."

**Moderator tip**: Push for specificity. "I use ChatGPT" isn't enough. "I use Claude to draft customer emails, then edit for tone" — that's useful.

### 4. The judgment question
"When has AI given you confidently wrong information? How do you know when to trust it and when to verify?"

**Moderator tip**: Critical thinking about AI output is the core skill of being AI native. This question surfaces it.

### 5. The adoption question
"If you had to teach a friend or colleague to become AI native in one week, what would you have them do? What's the minimum viable AI literacy?"

**Moderator tip**: End with actionable takeaways. This question generates a crowdsourced curriculum from the room.

## Localization prompt

> "In [city], AI adoption is [describe — e.g., 'racing ahead in the tech sector but barely touching traditional industries' / 'being mandated by employers who don't fully understand it' / 'something people are curious about but don't know where to start']. Tonight we're closing that gap — together."

## Audience participation ideas

- **Tool share**: Everyone writes their top 3 AI tools on a sticky note. Group them on a wall. See what the room actually uses
- **Live demo**: Ask 2–3 volunteers to show their actual AI workflows on screen. 3 minutes each
- **Prompt battle**: Give everyone the same task. 5 minutes to get the best AI output. Share and vote on results
- **Resource list**: Collaboratively build a "starter kit" of tools, tutorials, and prompts. Share it after the event
