# Track 6: AI in Your City & Industry

## The question

What does AI mean for the people, industries, and communities in *this* city?

## Opening framing (2 minutes — read or adapt)

> Global conversations about AI are important. But AI doesn't land globally. It lands locally. It lands in your hospital, your school, your factory, your newsroom, your city council.
>
> The impact of AI in [city] will be different from the impact in San Francisco or London or Lagos — because our economy is different, our workforce is different, our culture is different, and our challenges are different.
>
> Tonight we're making this local. What does AI mean for us, here, now?

## Discussion questions

### 1. The landscape question
"What industries define [city]? Which of them are being most affected by AI right now — positively or negatively?"

**Moderator tip**: Map the local economy first. Get the room thinking about their own backyard.

### 2. The stories question
"Does anyone here have a firsthand story of AI changing their workplace, school, or community? What happened?"

**Moderator tip**: Personal stories are the engine of this track. Give them time. Don't rush to the next question.

### 3. The opportunity question
"Where is the biggest untapped opportunity for AI in [city]? What problem could AI solve here that nobody's working on yet?"

**Moderator tip**: This is where the room can dream together. Civic problems, local industry challenges, community needs.

### 4. The risk question
"What's the biggest risk AI poses to [city] specifically? Is it job displacement in a key industry? Surveillance? Inequality? Something else?"

**Moderator tip**: Keep this grounded. "AI will destroy all jobs" is too vague. "AI might automate 30% of the roles at [local company]" is specific and real.

### 5. The action question
"If this room could start one AI initiative for [city] — a project, a policy, a program — what should it be?"

**Moderator tip**: End with agency. The room should leave feeling like they can *do something*, not just talk about it.

## How to localize this track

This is the most customizable track. Here's how to make it yours:

### Research your city (30 minutes of prep)

1. **Top industries**: What are the 3–5 biggest employers or sectors?
2. **AI companies**: Are there AI startups, research labs, or tech companies based here?
3. **University activity**: Is a local university doing AI research? What focus areas?
4. **Government**: Has your city/region published an AI strategy or policy?
5. **News**: Search "[city] + artificial intelligence" — what comes up?

### Tailor the opening

Replace the generic framing with specifics:

> "In [city], [X industry] employs [N] people. Last month, [specific event — a company announced AI adoption, a university launched an AI program, a local government published a policy]. Tonight we're asking what this means for us."

### Invite local voices

This track works best when panelists are:
- A local business leader navigating AI adoption
- A worker in an industry being transformed by AI
- A local government official or policy person
- A university researcher or educator
- A community organizer or nonprofit leader

## Audience participation ideas

- **Local mapping**: Draw a map of the city on a whiteboard. Have people place sticky notes where AI is already showing up (companies, schools, hospitals, government). See the pattern
- **Industry breakouts**: Split the room by industry. Each group discusses: "How is AI affecting your field in [city]?" Report back
- **Letter to the mayor**: Collectively draft a 1-paragraph letter to local government about what this city needs regarding AI. Actually send it
