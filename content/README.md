# Content

Session content for Humans in AI Week events.

## Discussion tracks

Every HAIW event centers on one or more of these six tracks. Each guide includes opening framing, 5 discussion questions, moderator tips, and a localization prompt.

| # | Track | Best for |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | [What It Means to Be Human in the AI Era](./discussion-guides/track-1-human-in-ai-era.md) | General audiences, philosophical communities |
| 2 | [AI and the Future of Work](./discussion-guides/track-2-future-of-work.md) | Business communities, career-focused audiences |
| 3 | [AI Safety & Governance](./discussion-guides/track-3-safety-and-governance.md) | Policy-oriented, academic, civic communities |
| 4 | [Building with AI](./discussion-guides/track-4-building-with-ai.md) | Technical communities, developers, builders |
| 5 | [How to Be AI Native](./discussion-guides/track-5-ai-native.md) | Mixed audiences, early adopters, professionals |
| 6 | [AI in Your City & Industry](./discussion-guides/track-6-ai-in-your-city.md) | Any chapter — the most localizable track |

## How to pick your track

- **First-time host?** Start with Track 1 or Track 6 — they're the most accessible.
- **Technical community?** Track 4 or Track 5.
- **Want to go deep?** Track 3 pairs well with academic or policy speakers.
- **Mix and match**: The hybrid event format (Format C in the playbook) lets you run a panel on one track, then breakout roundtables on different tracks.
