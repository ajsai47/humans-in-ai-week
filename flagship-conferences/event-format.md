# Flagship Event Format & Agenda

Full-day conference format for flagship HAIW events in hub cities.

---

## Format overview

The flagship is a **single-day, multi-track conference** with a main stage and breakout sessions. It runs 9 AM – 6 PM with an optional evening reception/afterparty.

**Vibe reference**: AI+ Renaissance (2,000 ppl, keynotes + demos + networking), but centered on the HAIW thesis — "where are we going with AI, and who gets a say?" — not product launches.

The flagship should feel like the grown-up version of the chapter meetup: same questions, bigger stage, higher-signal speakers, more structured networking.

---

## Suggested agenda

### Main stage (full day)

| Time | Segment | Duration | Notes |
|------|---------|----------|-------|
| 8:00 AM | **Registration + coffee** | 60 min | Badges, sponsor booths, networking |
| 9:00 AM | **Opening keynote** | 30 min | AIC founder or marquee speaker. Set the tone: why HAIW, why now, why this room |
| 9:30 AM | **Panel 1: The State of AI** | 45 min | Where are we? Big picture. 4 speakers from frontier labs, major tech, startups |
| 10:15 AM | **Break** | 15 min | |
| 10:30 AM | **Panel 2: AI & the Human Experience** | 45 min | Maps to Track 1 (Human in AI Era). Philosophers, creators, affected communities |
| 11:15 AM | **Fireside chat** | 30 min | Marquee 1-on-1. CEO, researcher, or policymaker. Intimate, honest conversation |
| 11:45 AM | **Lightning talks: AI in [City]** | 30 min | 5 x 6-min talks from local builders. What's being built here. |
| 12:15 PM | **Lunch** | 75 min | Sponsored. Networking. Partner demos in the hall |
| 1:30 PM | **Panel 3: AI Safety & Governance** | 45 min | Maps to Track 3. Policymakers, researchers, ethicists |
| 2:15 PM | **Breakout sessions (3 parallel)** | 60 min | See breakout format below |
| 3:15 PM | **Break** | 15 min | |
| 3:30 PM | **Panel 4: Building the Future** | 45 min | Maps to Track 4/5. Builders, founders, CTOs. What's working, what's hype |
| 4:15 PM | **Demo showcase** | 45 min | 6–8 startups, 5 min each. Curated, not pitch competition |
| 5:00 PM | **Closing keynote** | 30 min | The "what now?" talk. Synthesize the day. Call to action |
| 5:30 PM | **Closing remarks** | 10 min | Thank sponsors, speakers, attendees. Announce post-event report |
| 5:40 PM | **Networking reception** | 90 min | Drinks, food, open conversation. This is where deals happen |
| 7:10 PM | **Venue closes** | — | Optional: afterparty at a nearby venue |

### Breakout sessions (3 parallel, 60 min each)

Run during the 2:15 PM slot. Attendees choose one.

| Room | Track | Format | Capacity |
|------|-------|--------|----------|
| Room A | AI & the Future of Work | Moderated roundtable (8 tables of 10–12) | 80–120 |
| Room B | How to Be AI Native | Workshop — live demos + audience practice | 80–120 |
| Room C | AI in [City] | Open discussion with local leaders | 80–120 |

Breakout facilitators use the same [discussion guides](../content/discussion-guides/) as chapter meetups — adapted for the flagship audience.

## Stage programming principles

1. **No sales pitches from stage.** Sponsors get branding, booths, and a thank-you. Not a keynote slot.
2. **Diverse panels.** Every panel should have diversity across gender, race, industry, and AI experience level. No all-male, all-tech panels.
3. **Real conversation > rehearsed talks.** Moderators should push for honest answers, not talking points. Share questions in advance but not answers.
4. **Audience participation.** At least 2 sessions should include live Q&A or audience polls. People came to participate, not just watch.
5. **Local flavor.** The lightning talks and breakout sessions should be specific to the city. What's being built here? What are the local stakes?

## Content integration with chapter meetups

The flagship and chapter meetups are part of the same story. Reinforce this:

- Reference the 200+ chapter meetups happening worldwide during HAIW week
- Livestream the opening and closing keynotes so chapters can watch
- Use the same discussion tracks — the flagship just goes deeper
- Collect the same post-event data (themes, quotes) so the global narrative is unified

## Afterparty

Optional but recommended. Partner with a sponsor to host at a nearby venue.

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Time | 7:30 PM – 10:00 PM |
| Venue | Bar, restaurant, or event space within walking distance |
| Capacity | 200–500 (not full conference — creates exclusivity) |
| Access | VIP ticket holders + invite-only |
| Cost | Sponsor-funded |
| Vibe | Relaxed, music, drinks, conversation. Not a formal event |
