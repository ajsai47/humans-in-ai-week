# Track 4: Building with AI

## The question

How do you actually build useful things with AI — and what should you build?

## Opening framing (2 minutes — read or adapt)

> Two years ago, building with AI required a PhD and a GPU cluster. Today, anyone with a laptop and an API key can build an AI-powered product in a weekend. The barrier to entry has collapsed.
>
> But capability isn't the same as wisdom. Just because you *can* build something doesn't mean you *should*. And just because a demo works doesn't mean it's production-ready.
>
> Tonight is for builders. We're going to talk about what's actually working, what's hype, and how to build AI products that are useful, ethical, and real.

## Discussion questions

### 1. The stack question
"What's your current AI stack? What models, tools, and frameworks are you actually using in production — not just experimenting with?"

**Moderator tip**: This is a knowledge-sharing question. Let practitioners talk. Non-technical attendees learn a lot from hearing what real stacks look like.

### 2. The failure question
"What's an AI feature or product you built that didn't work? What did you learn?"

**Moderator tip**: Failure stories are gold. They're more instructive than success stories and they build trust in the room.

### 3. The evaluation question
"How do you know if your AI product is actually good? What metrics matter beyond 'it generates plausible output'?"

**Moderator tip**: Push past vibes-based evaluation. Accuracy, latency, cost, user retention, task completion — these matter.

### 4. The ethics question
"What's something you decided *not* to build with AI, and why?"

**Moderator tip**: This question separates thoughtful builders from move-fast-break-things builders. Celebrate the restraint.

### 5. The opportunity question
"What's an AI application that nobody's building yet but should be? What's the gap?"

**Moderator tip**: Great closer. This is where the room generates ideas together. Some of the best products start as bar napkin conversations.

## Localization prompt

> "In [city], the builder community is [describe — e.g., 'deeply focused on fintech' / 'one of the fastest-growing startup ecosystems in the region' / 'home to three major AI research labs']. The AI products being built here reflect that — [examples]. Tonight we're sharing what's working and what we're learning."

## Format note

This track works especially well as a **workshop + discussion** (Format C). Consider:
- 45-min hands-on session: build something small with AI (chatbot, classifier, agent)
- 45-min panel/discussion on the questions above
- Requires: Wi-Fi, laptops, pre-selected tools/tutorials

## Audience participation ideas

- **Live build**: If doing workshop format, have everyone build the same thing in 30 minutes. Share results
- **Show and tell**: 3-minute lightning demos from audience members on AI projects they've built
- **Stack vote**: Write common AI tools on the wall. Dot-vote on what people are actually using vs. what they've heard of
