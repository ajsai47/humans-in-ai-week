# Speaker Strategy

How to source, invite, and manage speakers for flagship HAIW conferences.

---

## Speaker profile

Flagship speakers come from **AIC's partner network** — 100+ companies spanning AI startups, frontier labs, enterprise, and research. The speaker bench is one of AIC's key advantages over community-run conferences like AI+.

### What we're looking for

| Role | Examples | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| **Startup founders/CEOs** | AI-native company leaders, series A–D founders | Building the future — real product stories |
| **Research leaders** | Lab directors, principal researchers at frontier labs | Technical depth + frontier perspective |
| **Enterprise AI leaders** | CTO/VP AI at Fortune 500 | How AI lands in the real economy |
| **Policy & governance** | Government AI advisors, think tank leaders, regulators | The governance conversation needs official voices |
| **Non-tech voices** | Educators, healthcare leaders, journalists, artists | The HAIW differentiator — not just builders |
| **Investors** | AI-focused VCs, corporate venture | Market perspective, funding trends |

### Diversity requirements

Every main stage panel must have:
- No more than 60% of any single gender
- At least one speaker from a non-technical background
- At least one speaker under 35

This isn't a nice-to-have. It's how the event reflects the HAIW thesis: the conversation includes everyone.

## Speaker pipeline

### Step 1: Build the target list (8–10 weeks out)

For each flagship city, build a list of 80–100 target speakers. Sources:

1. **AIC partner network** — primary source. Pull from the 100+ partner company contacts
2. **HumanX alumni** — previous HumanX speakers and attendees
3. **Local ecosystem** — founders, researchers, and leaders based in the flagship city
4. **Press/media** — journalists who cover AI (they'll also promote)
5. **Government/policy** — especially important for the DC flagship

### Step 2: Prioritize and tier

| Tier | Who | # per city | Outreach by |
|------|-----|-----------|-------------|
| **Headliners** | Marquee names — CEO/CTO of well-known AI companies, prominent researchers | 3–5 | AJ Green / Stephen Campbell |
| **Main stage** | Strong speakers with real expertise and stage presence | 15–20 | AIC partnerships team |
| **Breakout / Lightning** | Local builders, emerging voices, practitioners | 10–15 | City lead + chapter leads |

### Step 3: Invite (6–8 weeks out)

Use these templates, customized per speaker:

**For headliners** (personal email from AJ or Stephen):

> Subject: Speak at Humans in AI Week — [City] flagship, June [date]
>
> [Name],
>
> I'm personally inviting you to speak at the [City] flagship event for Humans in AI Week — a global initiative we're running across 200+ cities during June 1–7.
>
> The [City] flagship is a 1,500-person, full-day conference. Not a product expo — a serious conversation about where AI is going, featuring speakers from [list 2–3 confirmed names if possible].
>
> We'd love you for [specific slot — keynote / fireside / panel on X]. It's a [duration] commitment on [date].
>
> [One sentence on why them specifically.]
>
> Would love to make this happen.
>
> AJ

**For main stage speakers** (partnerships team):

> Subject: Speaker invitation — HAIW [City] Conference, June [date]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> We're hosting a Humans in AI Week flagship conference in [City] on [date] — 1,500 attendees, full-day, speakers from [name-drop 2–3 confirmed].
>
> We'd love to have you on our [panel name] panel discussing [topic]. It's a 45-minute moderated discussion with [other panelists if confirmed].
>
> No slides needed — just your perspective and experience. We'll share questions in advance.
>
> Interested? Happy to share more details.

### Step 4: Confirm and prep (4 weeks out)

Once confirmed:
1. Send confirmation email with date, time, venue, format, and what to expect
2. Share panel questions and co-panelist bios (2 weeks before)
3. Collect headshot + 2-sentence bio for the event page
4. Coordinate travel/accommodation if needed (headliners only)
5. Brief moderators on each speaker's background and what to ask them

### Step 5: Day-of speaker management

- Assign a speaker liaison (1 person per 5–6 speakers)
- Green room with water, coffee, phone chargers, and quiet space
- Brief speakers 15 minutes before their session
- Walk speakers to stage, mic check, introduce to moderator
- Thank speakers individually after their session

## Speaker logistics

| Item | Policy |
|------|--------|
| **Honorarium** | None — we don't pay speakers. The platform and network is the value. |
| **Travel** | Covered for headliner keynote speakers only ($500–$2,000 budget per speaker). All others are local or self-funded. |
| **Accommodation** | 1 night hotel for out-of-town headliners only |
| **Comp tickets** | Every speaker gets 2 comp VIP tickets |
| **VIP dinner** | Headliners and select main stage speakers are invited to the VIP dinner |
| **Recording consent** | All speakers must consent to video/audio recording. Include in confirmation email |

## Speaker page

Create a public speakers page on the event site (or in the Luma description) with:
- Headshot
- Name and title
- Company
- One-line bio
- Session they're speaking on

Update this page as speakers are confirmed — it drives registrations. Every new name is a reason to post on social.
