# Facilitation Tips

How to moderate a great HAIW conversation.

## Before the event

- **Know your discussion guide cold.** Read it 3 times. You don't need to memorize it, but you should be able to riff without reading from a sheet.
- **Talk to your panelists beforehand.** A 15-minute call a week before prevents 90% of day-of problems. Share the questions. Ask what they're most passionate about. Let them know you'll keep things moving.
- **Prepare follow-up questions.** For every main question, have 2–3 follow-ups ready: "Can you give an example?" / "Does anyone disagree?" / "How does that land for people in [local industry]?"

## During the event

### Setting the tone (first 5 minutes)

The first 5 minutes determine the entire event. You need to:

1. **Name the format**: "This is a conversation, not a lecture. I'm going to ask questions, our panelists will respond, and then we're opening it up to you."
2. **Set ground rules**: Read them from the [Run of Show template](../chapter-playbook/run-of-show-template.md). They matter.
3. **Lower the barrier**: "You don't need to be an AI expert to participate. If you use a phone, you're already in an AI relationship."

### Managing the conversation

- **Ask, don't tell.** Your job is to draw out insights, not share your own opinions. Save your takes for after.
- **Follow the energy.** If a question lands flat, move on. If the room lights up, let it run. The guide is a map, not a script.
- **Bridge between speakers.** "That connects to what [name] said earlier about X — do you agree?"
- **Protect quiet voices.** "I want to hear from someone who hasn't spoken yet." Look for people leaning forward or nodding — they want to talk but need permission.
- **Redirect politely.** If someone is monologuing: "That's a great point — let me get a few other perspectives on that."
- **Time-check often.** "We have 10 minutes left on this question, then we're moving to audience Q&A."

### Handling difficult moments

| Situation | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Someone dominates the conversation | "Thank you — I want to make sure we hear from others too. Who else has a perspective?" |
| A panelist goes off-topic | "Interesting — let me bring us back to [the question]. How does that connect?" |
| Audience question is actually a speech | "What's the question in there? Let's make sure our panelists can respond." |
| Someone says something controversial | Don't react. Ask: "That's a strong position. Does anyone see it differently?" Let the room self-correct. |
| Technical jargon is losing the room | "Can you explain that for someone who's not in tech? I think a lot of us would benefit." |
| Awkward silence | Count to 7 in your head. Someone will speak. If not: "Let me rephrase that..." |

### Ending well (last 5 minutes)

- **Synthesize**: "Here's what I heard tonight: [3 themes]. Did I miss anything?"
- **Name the impact**: "We just had a conversation that's happening in 200 cities this week. That matters."
- **Give a CTA**: "If you want to stay involved, [join our chapter / follow us / sign up for the newsletter]."
- **Thank everyone**: Sponsors, panelists, venue, attendees. Be genuine.
